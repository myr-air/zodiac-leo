# Lyric Craft And Multilingual Guardrails

Use for local lyric source work only.

Method: prefer concrete object plus action over abstract mood. Use small ordinary comparisons, compression, conversational phrasing, image turns, title payoff, deliberate per-section rhyme, and subtitle-ready line lengths.

Episode Style & Theme Spine gate: before any episode song slate or lyric drafting, create or consume the episode spine first. The spine must define listener job, theme thesis, base sonic lane, BPM range, vocal lane, core instrumentation, allowed variation slots, motif/lexical budgets, and safety boundaries. Every new or revised track must include a Track Delta that states how it differs from the spine without redefining the episode: story function, object/action function, macro-form or rhetorical delta, style/BPM delta, and motif/lexical delta against the previous tracks.

Story/reference gate: before lyric drafting, create a compact track story brief plus a 2-3 song reference triangle that fits the Episode Style & Theme Spine and its Track Delta. Real song/artist names may appear only in this internal source brief as inspiration anchors, never in Suno fields, lyrics, titles, metadata hooks, or provider prompts. Extract only abstract traits such as tempo feel, density, intimacy level, hook function, arrangement restraint, and mood arc. Do not copy or request melody, chords, lyrics, vocal tone, artist style, exact arrangement, or sound-alike behavior.

Title/story gate: song titles should come from story objects, actions, places, or emotional turns. Do not require instrument names such as piano, sax, guitar, or drums in titles just because the arrangement uses them. Instrument names belong in arrangement notes or style fields unless the instrument is a real diegetic object in the scene and central to the story.

Title/lyric independence gate: the title does not have to appear in the lyrics, and it must not automatically become the first line of the hook. A title may act as a mood label, scene label, object label, or after-the-fact summary. If the title appears in lyrics, its placement must be justified by hook function; otherwise prefer non-title hook lines, implied title imagery, or a final image that earns the title indirectly.

Lexical freshness gate: before drafting or approval, scan the previous approved/proposed tracks for repeated high-salience words and AI-ish comfort words. Keep a per-track lexical avoid/budget list. Do not lean on the same nouns, verbs, adjectives, or payoff words unless they are necessary scene objects. Current overuse risks include `soft`, `quiet`, `small`, `little`, `gentle`, `warm`, `slow`, `line`, `sign`, `page`, `name`, `same`, `tomorrow`, `smile`, `light`, `street`, `door`, `glass`, `hand`, `walk`, `wait`, `close`, `after school`, plus generic filler such as `glow`, `dream`, `vibe`, `cosmic`, `destiny`. Reuse of a necessary object is allowed only with a new action or image function.

Lexical count ledger gate: for future episodes and new/revised tracks, record a compact per-song count table before approval. Count lowercase/lemma-like high-salience nouns, pronouns, adjectives/adverbs, color words, object words, and hook/payoff terms from the title plus sung lyric text. Include repeated ordinary tokens that look harmless but accumulate across a set, such as `tray`, `blue`, `ink`, `you`, `my`, `your`, `soft`, or `warm`. Keep per-track counts and cumulative episode counts, then set the next-track budget: nonessential repeated terms should trend downward, usually capped at 0-1 uses in the next song if they already dominate the previous song or appear in multiple recent tracks. Do not retroactively rewrite an already approved episode only to satisfy the ledger; use the counts as a carryover lesson for the next episode unless Mayr/user explicitly requests revisions.

Macro-form guardrail: every song must have a structure fingerprint before drafting or approval. Do not let adjacent songs reuse the same full macro-structure by default. Vary section order and function with options such as hook-first, verse/refrain, AABA, call-and-response, short chorus plus instrumental answer, refrain-only chorus, two-part bridge, spoken/near-whisper break, instrumental-only bridge, or through-composed light narrative. If a song shares the previous track's structure, revise the macro-form while preserving title, hook, and core story.

Micro-pattern guardrail: every draft and approval needs a rhetorical fingerprint, not only section labels. Reject set-level sameness such as automatic title repetition twice inside the same chorus/refrain block, repeated `No ..., no ...` negative pairs, repeated `one small/soft note/sign/line` payoff formulas, identical final-hook stacking, repeated bridge openers like `Maybe...` or `No big...`, or four adjacent songs that all state the title in the same position. A song may repeat its title only when the repetition has a distinct musical function; otherwise vary the hook by answer line, image turn, call-back, instrumental answer, or non-title payoff.

Set-level review guardrail: before approving a track, compare it against at least the previous three approved/proposed songs when available. Make a compact matrix of macro-form, hook placement, title count per block, negative-construction count, bridge opener, final payoff, and repeated nouns/metaphors. If two or more adjacent songs share the same micro-pattern, return `REVISE` even if the section labels differ.

Lexical review guardrail: before approval, report the top repeated nouns/verbs/adjectives shared with at least the previous three tracks plus the lexical count ledger for the current song. Return `REVISE` if a new song repeats an existing high-salience word as a hook/payoff without a new scene function, if its repeated pronouns/adjectives/object words do not trend down after being budgeted, or if the lyric relies on generic AI comfort words instead of concrete objects/actions.

Reference-safety review guardrail: before approval, verify the reference triangle was transformed into original abstract traits and did not leak named song/artist references into final lyrics, Suno Styles, Exclude Styles, title, or metadata. Also scan final titles and high-salience lyric phrases for accidental named-reference collisions with well-known artists/songs; if a phrase can reasonably read as a named act/title rather than the scene object, return `REVISE` and retitle/rephrase. Return `REVISE` for imitation wording such as `like <artist>`, `in the style of <song>`, copied signature lyric phrasing, copied melodic/chord directions, or any sound-alike target.

Rhyme guardrail: every sung section needs a deliberate rhyme strategy. Prefer natural end rhyme/slant rhyme, internal rhyme, alliteration, and hook echoes. Reject weak/no rhyme in consecutive sections, forced rhyme, nursery-rhyme phrasing, or rhyme that makes the line less natural.

S01E01 full-set lessons gate: before approving a new Mellow Longplay draft, collect the active review issues from the prior tracks and treat them as a living issue ledger, not a vague style preference. The 13-track S01E01 review exposed recurring blockers that must be checked explicitly: repeated title-first chorus blocks; repeated invitation hooks such as `Save...`; forbidden understatement templates such as `Not a promise...`, `No big...`, `No ..., no ...`, `Maybe...`, and `Nothing...`; repeated full final chorus stacking; adjacent songs with the same `Intro → Verse → Hook/Chorus → Verse → Instrumental → Bridge → Final Hook` shape; repeated final-only refrain plus object-coda shape; weak orphan end-words in pre-hooks/bridges; repeated `You say / I say` dialogue as a crutch; and object-token-as-secret-proof reuse without a new function. Return `REVISE` when a draft repeats one of these patterns without a clear new musical or scene function.

Object-function gate: concrete objects are not enough by themselves. Each title object should do a different job in the sequence: scene anchor, practical marker, shared task, route choice, study aid, closing sound, etc. Reject adjacent songs where different objects all perform the same emotional function, such as quiet proof, secret promise, saved seat, or delayed confession.

Track Delta review gate: do not PASS a track that only restates the episode base style/theme. The delta must be controlled and audible/readable: one or two focused changes in object function, hook promise, section behavior, arrangement color, BPM, or motif role. Return `REVISE` if the delta breaks the episode spine, duplicates an adjacent track's object function, consumes a reserved variation slot without review, or leaves the song interchangeable with the previous tracks.

Reject generic AI filler, blocked glow/dream/vibe/cosmic-destiny language, repeated motifs without scene function, crowded syllables, unsafe imitation, sexualized minor framing, adult/minor romance, teacher/student romance, childlike vocal/sexualization, fake provenance, or rights/platform claims. Wholesome PG same-age teen/high-school first-love framing is allowed only when the user explicitly approves that episode direction.

For multilingual lines, include meaning brief, native line, literal gloss, usage, and native/fluent review requirement before provider use.

Provider use, generated audio, upload, publishing, Content ID, monetization, and platform-safety claims remain blocked.
