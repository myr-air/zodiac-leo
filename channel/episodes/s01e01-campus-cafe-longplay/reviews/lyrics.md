## Track Notes

### 1. Coffee Rings on My Notes

- Strength: opens the episode with a tangible cafe table and a first-noticing moment.
- Watch later: `cup`, `page`, and `afternoon` appear often by design; keep later tracks object-specific to avoid sameness.

### 2. Seat by the Window

- Strength: turns repeated location into routine without making a big promise too early.
- Watch later: chorus line `not a promise, not a plan` fits the restraint; avoid reusing that logic in nearby songs.

### 3. Your Pen on the Table

- Strength: the borrowed pen gives the first direct interaction a concrete reason.
- Watch later: bridge is a little more direct emotionally; keep track 4 quieter and piano-forward as planned.

### 4. Quiet Page Turns

- Strength: lowers the dynamic and gives the piano-forward song a clear reason to be sparse.
- Watch later: page/reading imagery overlaps with earlier notes; avoid making track 7 another reading or notebook song.

### 5. Half-Smile Refill

- Strength: introduces cafe motion and call-response without making the relationship too large too soon.
- Watch later: cup/refill wording is acceptable here, but future tracks should move away from drink imagery.

### 6. Library Card in Your Case

- Strength: expands the world into campus life with a specific object outside the table/cup cluster.
- Watch later: keep the next campus detail practical and new, not another card/book/library item.

### 7. Blue Straw, Soft Foam

- Strength: adds a playful, brighter texture and shifts the hook from cup/table language to straw/lid/map actions.
- Revision response: changed `warm foam` to `soft foam` after Gemini flagged the tactile phrase.

### 8. Two Plugs, One Outlet

- Strength: turns study logistics into teamwork with a clear practical object and low-key intimacy.
- Watch later: keep the teamwork metaphor practical, not too romantic or slogan-like.

### 9. Saxophone from the Street

- Strength: delivers the planned soft-sax accent and briefly opens the cafe world to the street without changing genre.
- Watch later: keep the sax as a color accent only; do not drift into busy cafe-jazz soloing.

### 10. Rain Check by the Door

- Strength: creates the first clear almost-plan for another day without forcing a confession.
- Revision response: chorus now foregrounds `By the door` and uses `quiet rain check` as a smaller phrase to reduce pun-led feel.

### 11. Backpack by My Chair

- Strength: makes trust spatial and understated through the backpack/charm object.
- Watch later: the gold crescent is only a lyric object here; do not treat it as media provenance or rights evidence.

### 12. Last Light on the Counter

- Strength: closes the main set with a warm time-of-day image and wider outro.
- Watch later: light/afternoon imagery is repeated by design; if the episode feels too samey, tighten this lyric first.

### 13. Walk Me Past the Courtyard

- Strength: gives the bonus track a complete walk-away close and gently reprises the restrained emotional language.
- Revision response: bridge now uses specific episode callbacks (`crooked map`, `receipt`, `blue-ink note`) instead of repeating `No big confession / No perfect line` language.
