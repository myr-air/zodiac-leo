# S01E01 Lyric Antipattern Registry

Status: active source-only rolling guardrail  
Updated: 2026-05-19

## Boundary

This registry records lyric-writing antipatterns for future source drafts. It does not approve provider/browser/API/account action, generated media, render/export, upload/publish, or rights/platform claims.

## Global Antipatterns

- Do not force the title into the lyric, hook, or first chorus line.
- Do not use instrument names as titles unless the instrument is a story object.
- Avoid repeated title blocks, `Title / image / Title / payoff`, final hook stacking, `No ..., no ...`, `No big...`, `Maybe...`, `Nothing...`, and `one small/soft note/sign/line` payoffs.
- Avoid repeated generic comfort words unless scene-required: `soft`, `quiet`, `small`, `little`, `gentle`, `warm`, `slow`, `line`, `sign`, `page`, `name`, `same`, `tomorrow`, `smile`, `light`, `street`, `door`, `glass`, `hand`, `walk`, `wait`, `close`, `after school`, `glow`, `dream`, `vibe`, `cosmic`, `destiny`.
- Future episodes must keep a Lexical Count Ledger per song. Count high-salience nouns, pronouns, modifiers, color/object words, and hook/payoff terms; user-observed S01E01 examples include `tray`, `blue`, and `ink`. Use the ledger to lower nonessential repeats in the following songs instead of only relying on subjective freshness notes.
- Use real-song references only in Story + Reference Briefs; reduce them to abstract traits and keep names out of lyrics/Suno fields.
