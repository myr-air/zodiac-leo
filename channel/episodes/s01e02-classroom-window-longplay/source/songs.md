### Track 1 — Chalk Dust on the Window Rail

Status: source-approved v1.0 / source-only / no provider or media approval

```text
[Intro]
Chalk wakes under afternoon air
On the rail by the window, your sleeve leaves it there

[Verse 1]
Back row hums, chair feet on tile
Your cuff lifts chalk from a crooked pile
You laugh at the mark along your sleeve
Then angle your notes so the clock can breathe

[Pre-Chorus]
I tuck my questions under the date
Yours make the lecture turn its weight
Two raised pens hold, then move along
I learn the measure you're leaning on

[Chorus]
I catch what you're noticing
Before the hour moves on
The paper turns toward an answer
Not quite drawn
If care begins in attention
I can keep it calm
And leave the words unspoken
As the lesson moves along

[Verse 2]
Side desks click with zipper sounds
Your coffee cools as the clock comes round
You square the eraser beside your book
I lose my sentence to one quick look

The campus trees cross over the board
Your question lands where mine was stored
You turn one corner for me to see
I keep the answer half-known with me

[Chorus]
I catch what you're noticing
Before the hour moves on
The paper turns toward an answer
Not quite drawn
If care begins in attention
I can keep it calm
And leave the words unspoken
As the lesson moves along

[Bridge]
Halfway through the hour, dust settles down
Your sleeve leaves a crescent, pale and round
I nearly ask what you read after class
Then hold my breath and let the lecture pass

[Final Refrain]
I catch what you're noticing
Before the hour moves on
If care begins in attention
I can keep it calm

[Outro]
On the rail, a chalk trace stays
A classroom edge in afternoon haze
Where my first question changed its shape
When your sleeve brushed dust away
```
