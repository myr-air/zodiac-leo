## Boundary

This registry records lyric-writing antipatterns for future source drafts. It does not approve provider/browser/API/account action, generated media, render/export, upload/publish, or rights/platform claims.

## Global Antipatterns

- Do not force the title into the lyric, hook, or first chorus line.
- Do not use instrument names as titles unless the instrument is a diegetic story object.
- Avoid repeated title blocks, title/image/title/payoff chorus grids, final hook stacking, `No big...`, `Maybe...`, `Nothing...`, `No ..., no ...`, and `one small/soft note/sign/line` payoffs.
- Avoid generic comfort words unless scene-required: `soft`, `quiet`, `small`, `little`, `gentle`, `warm`, `slow`, `line`, `sign`, `page`, `name`, `same`, `tomorrow`, `smile`, `light`, `street`, `door`, `glass`, `hand`, `walk`, `wait`, `close`, `glow`, `dream`, `vibe`, `cosmic`, `destiny`.
- Avoid repeating S01E01-heavy words as emotional shortcuts: `tray`, `blue`, `ink`, `after school`, `save`, `promise`.
- Do not make every object a secret proof of affection; each object needs a real classroom/campus function.
- Do not make every song the same sub-theme with a different object. Each track needs a distinct mini-story, lyric direction, structure role, melody-contour direction, and rhythm/groove direction under the shared episode theme.
- Use real-song references only in Story + Reference Briefs as abstract traits; keep names out of lyrics, titles, Suno fields, metadata, and prompts.

## Batch Lexical Review Rule

For every proposed lyric, record:

- repeated high-salience nouns/object words against all earlier S01E02 tracks;
- repeated adjectives/adverbs, especially comfort modifiers (`soft`, `quiet`, `small`, `little`, `gentle`, `warm`, `slow`, `light`, `plain`, `calm`);
- pronoun load (`I`, `you`, `your`, `my`, `we`) when object/action can carry meaning;
- repeated hook/payoff terms (`notice`, `care`, `answer`, `question`, `same`, `tomorrow`, `wait`, `stay`, `almost`);
- the concrete revision made when a word repeats too often.

If a noun or adjective appears across adjacent songs without a new function, revise before PASS. If a term appears in 3+ songs across the batch, either reserve it as a deliberate motif with different actions or replace it.

## Batch Sub-Theme / Music-Shape Review Rule

For every proposed lyric, also record:

- sub-theme / mini-story lane and how it differs from the main theme and nearby songs;
- lyric direction: what the words are doing beyond repeating `curiosity becomes care`;
- structure fingerprint: section order, hook placement, bridge role, final section role;
- melody-contour direction: narrow/wide range, rising/falling hook, repeated phrase, reduced outro, instrumental answer space;
- rhythm/groove direction: BPM, bounce, rests, syncopation, half-time feel, sparse vs moving sections;
- light S01E01 comparison: flag repeated object function, hook grid, final stack, or ending gesture, but do not block normal cozy channel identity.

Return `REVISE` if adjacent tracks share the same sub-theme, object function, section shape, melody-contour plan, or groove behavior without a clear reason.
