## Episode Style & Theme Spine

- Listener job: Cozy golden hour longplay for afternoon wind-down, watching the sky, quiet desk/room breaks, and gentle twilight comfort.
- Theme thesis: The quiet transition of golden hour mirrors the unspoken comfort of just being there. Feelings are felt through the cool wind, the shifting colors of the sky, and the warm fading sun, completely stepping away from classrooms and study desks.
- Base `Styles` lane: cozy chill vocal, soft R&B, mellow city-pop / easy-listening harmony, comfortable female vocal, warm electric keys, clean guitar, rounded bass, soft brushed drums, airy pads, restrained chorus lift, subtitle-friendly lyric pacing.
- BPM lane: about 74-92 BPM overall; default middle 82-88 BPM; closers 74-80 BPM; one brighter lift may reach about 92 BPM.
- Vocal lane: natural adult/young-adult female vocal; no named voice, real-person imitation, or childlike framing.
- Base `Exclude Styles`: named-artist imitation, known-song imitation, real school or brand naming, trap, aggressive rap, EDM drop, hard rock edge, busy jazz soloing, orchestral drama, novelty/childlike vocal, sexualized campus framing, teacher-student romance, adult-minor framing, rights/platform claims.
- Control baseline: Weirdness about 10-14%; Style Influence 82% unless a track-specific review approves otherwise.
- Reserved variation slots: Track 5 features **Electric Vibraphone** as the single planned non-sax special instrument. It brings a soft, shimmering, bell-like warmth that perfectly captures the golden light reflecting off the city. Piano is used selectively as an intimate color on Track 9. No other spotlight instrument is allowed.
- Per-track field review must also confirm the song's sub-theme / mini-story, structure role, melody-contour plan, and rhythm/groove direction match `source/songs.md` and do not duplicate adjacent tracks.
